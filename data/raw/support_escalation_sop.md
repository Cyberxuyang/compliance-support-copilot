# Support Escalation SOP

## Purpose

This SOP defines when support agents can answer directly and when a case must be escalated for manager or compliance review.

## Direct Response Allowed

Support agents may answer directly when:

- The question is covered by an approved policy or FAQ.
- The customer has provided the required identifying information.
- The request does not involve fraud indicators, restricted products, legal advice, tax advice, or sensitive personal data.
- The answer can be supported by a cited internal document.

## Missing Information

If required information is missing, the agent should not provide a final decision. The agent should ask for the missing information and explain why it is needed.

For refund cases, required information includes order ID, purchase email address, purchase date, reason for refund, and product condition.

For claim cases, required information includes claim reference number, customer contact email, claim category, event date, and current case status if available.

## Manager Escalation

Manager escalation is required when:

- A refund is requested after the standard 14-day window.
- The customer cannot provide proof of purchase.
- The transaction cannot be verified.
- The customer requests an exception to a non-refundable item rule.
- The case involves repeated complaints about the same order.

## Compliance Escalation

Compliance escalation is required when:

- The request involves suspected fraud.
- The customer asks for legal, tax, or regulatory advice.
- The case involves restricted products.
- The customer provides sensitive personal data in an unsupported channel.
- The available documents do not provide enough evidence for a safe answer.

## Response Requirements

Every response should include:

- A concise answer
- The policy or SOP source used
- Any missing information
- Recommended next step
- Whether escalation is required

Agents should avoid unsupported promises, final legal interpretations, or statements that conflict with approved policy documents.
